Thusharagiri waterfalls  ------> Thusharagiri Waterfalls, located 50 km from Kozhikode in Kerala's Western Ghats, is a scenic three-tiered waterfall—Erattumukku, Mazhavil Chattom, and Thumbithullum Para—known for its mist-covered peaks and trekking trails. It offers a serene escape with lush green surroundings, suitable for trekking, nature walks, and, specifically in the lower pools, refreshing dips, particularly between June and November

Irapara ---> Aripara Falls (alternate spelling: Arippara Falls) is a tourist spot in Anakkampoyil near Thiruvambady Town in Kozhikode district, Kerala. It is located on the Thiruvambady – Anakkampoyil route, 15 km from Thiruvambadi. The waterfall is a tributary of Iruvanjippuzha. There is a proposal for implementing a hydro power project on the Aripara waterfall.

Vythiri View Point --> Vythiri View Point, often synonymous with the nearby Lakkidi View Point, offers breathtaking, panoramic vistas of Wayanad’s lush, mist-clad hills, deep gorges, and winding hairpin turns from 700 meters above sea level. It is a premier spot for witnessing stunning sunsets, dense rainforests, and dramatic, cloud-covered

En uru --> En Ooru Wayanad- Entry Fee, Visiting Time And Location ...En Ooru (En Uru) is Kerala's first tribal heritage village located in Pookode, Wayanad, designed to showcase the rich culture, traditions, and lifestyle of the indigenous communities. Spread across 25 acres of scenic hillocks, this 10-crore project features traditional huts, 16 handicraft stalls, an open-air theater for cultural performances, and a café serving ethnic cuisine

. Known as the highest altitude freshwater lake in the state, this 5–8.5 hectare, 6.5m-deep destination offers boating, a children's park, an aquarium, and a spice emporium



Honey Museum --> The BeeCraft Honey Museum in Wayanad, Kerala, established in 2021 as India’s first (and Asia's largest) honey museum, is a specialized educational destination in Old Vythiri. It offers an immersive experience into the world of bees, showcasing live demos, the honey-making process,, and various honey types, complete with tastings and a shop

Banasura sagar dam --> Located in Wayanad, Kerala, the Banasura Sagar Dam is India's largest and Asia's second-largest earthen dam. Constructed in 1979 across the Karamanathodu tributary of the Kabani River, it features massive, stacked boulder walls. It is a popular, scenic tourist spot known for its reservoir, speed boating, and trekking opportunities

Karaladu-- Karlad Lake is located in Thariode, Wayanad. It is the third largest fresh water lake in Kerala and second largest lake in Wayanad after Pookode Lake. The lake is 3 km away from Banasura Sagar Dam

900 kandi---> The 900 Kandi Glass Bridge in Wayanad is South India's first glass bridge, offering a 100-foot high,,thrilling,walk over panoramic,forest views. Located in Meppadi, this 40-meter-long,Italian-made fiberglass structure at Sky Park provides stunning 360-degree views of the Western Ghats, particularly the Chembra and Vellarimala peaks

Edakkal caves-->Edakkal Caves, located on Ambukutty Mala in Kerala's Wayanad district, are not actual caves but a 22-ft wide, 96-ft long natural rock shelter formed by a wedged boulder. Renowned for Neolithic/Mesolithic petroglyphs (circa 6000 B.C.) showcasing ancient human life, tools, and scripts, they represent one of India's earliest known human habitation

Tirunelli ---> The Thirunelli Mahavishnu Temple is a 1,000-year-old Hindu pilgrimage site in Kerala's Wayanad district, nestled in the Brahmagiri hills at 900m altitude. Known as the "Kashi of the South" (Sahyamala Kshetram), it is dedicated to Lord Vishnu, featuring traditional Kerala architecture, a sacred Papanasini stream for cleansing sins, and ancient stone aqueducts

Kuruva --> Uncovering Kuruva Island: Kerala's Pristine Ecological WondersKuruva Island (Kuruvadweep) is a 950-acre, uninhabited, and protected river delta in the Kabini River, Wayanad, Kerala, offering a serene escape filled with dense, evergreen forests, bamboo groves, and rare bird species. Accessible by bamboo rafts, it is a premier eco-tourism destination for nature walks, photography, and birdwatching, typically open from December to April

Tholpeety ---> Tholpetty Wildlife Sanctuary, located in the northern part of Wayanad, Kerala, near the Karnataka border, is a major, dense forest area and part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve. It is a renowned eco-tourism destination offering jeep safaris to spot wildlife like elephants, tigers, leopards, and bears in their natural habitat
