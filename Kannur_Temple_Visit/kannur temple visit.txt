

Kannur, known for its rich spiritual heritage and vibrant Theyyam rituals, boasts several ancient temples in North Malabar. Key highlights include the Parassinikadavu Muthappan Temple (famous for daily Theyyam), Rajarajeshwara Temple in Taliparamba, and the snake-worship site Sree Peralassery Temple. 


Parassinikadavu Muthappan Temple (18 km from Kannur): Located on the banks of the Valapattanam River, this temple is famous for worshipping Sree Muthappan, a Theyyam deity, and is unique for welcoming people of all faiths, offering fish and toddy [1, Kerala Tourism].

Rajarajeshwara Temple, Taliparamba: One of the oldest and most revered Shiva temples in Kerala, believed to be centuries old and historically significant.
Mridanga Saileswari Temple, Muzhakkunnu: Dedicated to Goddess Durga, this temple is considered a patron of music and arts.
Kalarivathukkal Bhagavathi Temple: A temple dedicated to Goddess Bhadrakali, closely associated with the martial art Kalaripayattu
